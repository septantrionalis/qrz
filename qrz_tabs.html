Just a quick note for CW operators trying to make contact with me. I feel comfortable rag chewing at around 20 WPM. I can do upwards of 27 WPM for quick exchanges if your send is good (e.g. POTA or contest exchanges). For a bit of history, I got licensed back in February of 2007 and obtained my general class license a few months later. It wasn't until 2022 that I upgraded to the extra class license. After falling out of amateur radio for a few years, I got back into it early 2022 thanks to the Parks on the Air program. This pushed me to learn morse code during the summer of 2022. As of early 2023, I operate strictly on CW.

SKCC #: 26627T

For those trying to remember my callsign, think "Zombie Pepper Spray" (KC0ZPS). By default, I don't send QSL cards for POTA contacts (hunting or activating), but if we do make a POTA exchange and you want one of my zombie pepper spray cards, reach out to me and I'll be more than happy to send you one!

My base station is an ICOM 7300, Hustler 6 band vertical antenna, and an Ameritron AL-811 amplifier. I also have an ICOM 208H for the truck, a Yaesu FTM-10R for the motorcycle, and a Yaesu VX-8R handheld transceiver when mobile on my feet.

My equipment of choice for POTA activations is an ICOM 705 using 10 watts with the CHA MPAS 2.0 in the vertical configuration or the Packtenna EFHW in the sloper configuration.
